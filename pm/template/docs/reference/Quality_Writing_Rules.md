# Quality Writing Rules

> **When to apply:** Any time Claude is asked to write something that will be shared with other people — forum posts, pitches, character backstories for submission, emails, messages. NOT for casual back-and-forth chat during brainstorming sessions. When in doubt, ask: "Want me to apply the writing rules for this?"

---

## AI Dead Giveaways — What to Avoid

### 1. The "Firstly, Secondly, In Conclusion" skeleton
AI loves rigid structure. Every paragraph neatly follows the last. Real humans meander, circle back, go on tangents. Don't outline your writing with visible scaffolding.

### 2. Em dashes everywhere
AI (especially Claude) overuses em dashes. Humans use them occasionally. AI drops them 3-4 times per paragraph. Limit to ONE per paragraph max. Use commas, periods, or parentheses instead.

### 3. Buzzword bingo
Words real people rarely use but AI can't stop saying. **Never use these:**

- "delve" / "delving into"
- "landscape" (as in "the AI landscape")
- "leverage" / "harness"
- "tapestry"
- "multifaceted"
- "navigate" (metaphorically)
- "robust"
- "foster"
- "holistic"
- "it's worth noting that..."
- "in today's rapidly evolving..."
- "nuanced"
- "cornerstone"
- "paradigm"
- "synergy"
- "whilst" (unless writing for a British audience)

### 4. Perfect parallel structure
Every bullet point starts the same way. Every sentence is roughly the same length. Humans are messy. Short sentence, then a run-on, then a fragment. Vary it.

### 5. No personality or opinion
AI hedges everything: "It could be argued that..." / "Many experts suggest..." Real people just say what they think. Pick a side.

### 6. The compliment sandwich
AI always balances positive and negative. "While X has its challenges, it also offers remarkable opportunities." Humans pick a side and run with it.

### 7. Zero specific details
AI says "many businesses have found success." A human says "my buddy Steve tried this at his shop on Queen Street and it doubled his walk-ins." Be specific. Names, places, numbers.

### 8. Suspiciously clean grammar
No contractions, no sentence fragments, no starting sentences with "And" or "But." Perfect punctuation everywhere. Real writing has texture and rough edges.

### 9. The Rule of Three
AI groups everything in threes. Three adjectives, three bullet points, three examples. "Brilliant, restless, and bored." Humans don't naturally package things so neatly. Use two sometimes. Or four. Or just one.

### 10. Over-explanation
AI explains things nobody asked about. "Tokyo, the capital of Japan, is a bustling city." Trust the reader. They know things. Don't condescend.

### 11. Too comprehensive
AI covers every angle, wraps every point with a bow, addresses every possible objection. Real writing leaves gaps. Not everything needs to be said.

## How to Make It Sound Human

### Voice and Rhythm
- **Break the rhythm.** Short sentence. Then a longer one that rambles a bit. Fragment. It should feel uneven.
- **Use contractions.** "Don't" not "do not." "It's" not "it is." "Can't" not "cannot."
- **Start sentences with And, But, So.** AI avoids this. Humans do it constantly.
- **Vary sentence length aggressively.** Don't let three sentences in a row be the same approximate length.

### Personality and Opinion
- **Have an opinion.** Don't hedge. Say "this is better" not "this could potentially be considered superior."
- **Add specific details.** Names, places, numbers, anecdotes. Even made-up-sounding specifics read as human.
- **Use casual language where appropriate.** "This thing is kind of wild" reads more human than "This development is particularly noteworthy."
- **Leave room for personality.** Parenthetical asides, a joke, an incomplete thought. Things only a specific person would say.

### Imperfection
- **Leave in mild imperfections.** A slightly awkward transition. A thought that doesn't quite land. That's how people actually write.
- **Kill the transitions.** Remove "Furthermore," "Moreover," "Additionally," "In conclusion," "It's important to note." Just say the next thing.
- **Don't wrap everything up neatly.** Not every paragraph needs a concluding sentence. Sometimes you just stop.

### The Read-Aloud Test
Read it out loud. If it sounds like a TED talk script, it's too AI. If it sounds like you talking to a friend, you're closer. If it sounds like a real person wrote it in 20 minutes instead of a machine generating it in 2 seconds, you nailed it.

---

## The Single Biggest Tell

AI writing is too smooth. Human writing has friction, personality, and rough edges. The goal isn't perfection. The goal is authenticity.

When producing any writing that will be shared:
1. Write the draft
2. Re-read it looking for the giveaways listed above
3. Break some of the structure
4. Add voice and rough edges
5. Cut the fancy words
6. Make it sound like a person wrote it

---

## Quick Checklist Before Submitting

- [ ] Em dash count: 0-1 per paragraph, not more
- [ ] No buzzwords from the banned list
- [ ] Sentence lengths vary noticeably
- [ ] At least one contraction per paragraph
- [ ] No "Firstly/Secondly/In conclusion" structure
- [ ] Has a specific detail or two (name, place, number)
- [ ] Doesn't cover every possible angle — leaves gaps
- [ ] Starts at least one sentence with And, But, or So
- [ ] Doesn't group things in perfect threes every time
- [ ] Read it out loud — does it sound like a person?
